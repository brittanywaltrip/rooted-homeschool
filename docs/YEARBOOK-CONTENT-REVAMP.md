# Yearbook content & keepsake revamp

Brittany's vision: move the yearbook from "a record of the school year" to "a keepsake
of childhood." Warmer, book-like, emotional, written so a family reads it together in
ten or twenty years. This doc captures the new wording and structure, sequenced into
buildable waves.

## One guardrail up front (no AI)
Brittany's "weave the twelve monthly answers into a narrative" idea must NOT use AI
(project rule: Rooted uses no AI). The keepsake version is BETTER anyway: present the
twelve real answers in a beautifully designed month-by-month spread, in the family's
own words. Real words beat generated prose for a keepsake. Anywhere this doc says
"narrative" or "story," it means a designed template of their actual entries, never
machine-written text.

## New table of contents (book-like)
Replace "Zoe's Chapter" etc. with real book sections:
- A Letter From Home
- Zoe's Story
- Emma's Story
- Adventures Together
- Family Traditions
- Looking Back

## Cover subtitle
Replace "2025–2026" with an evocative line (let the family pick, default the first):
- "A year of growing, wondering, and becoming."
- "Our homeschool story."
- "The memories behind the lessons."
(Keep the year somewhere small; lead with the line.)

## Letter From Home (guided, not blank)
Replace "A message from the heart" with a guided prompt:
> Dear Future Us… Imagine reading this five or ten years from now. Tell the story of
> this homeschool year. What surprised you? What challenged your family? What made you
> proud? What moments do you never want to forget?
Sign-off: "Love, The {Family} Family" (already using family name; add "Love,").

## A Day We'll Never Forget (was "Favorite Moment")
Rename to "A Day We'll Never Forget." Fields: Date · Location · What happened · Why
we'll always remember it. Photo caption prompts (replace "it just does"):
- "What made today special?"
- "What happened that made everyone smile?"
Plus: "Favorite quote from that day."

## This Was {Child} (new keepsake snapshot — year-over-year compare)
A per-child snapshot page so families can compare every year:
Age · Grade · Height · Favorite Color · Dream Job · Current Obsession · Favorite Snack
· Best Friend · Signature Phrase · What Makes Me Laugh.

## Year-End Conversation (was the interview — new questions)
- What made you happiest this year?
- What made you brave?
- What was really hard?
- What surprised you?
- What made you laugh until your stomach hurt?
- If you could relive one day, which would it be?
- What are you most proud of?
- Who helped you this year?
- What's something Mom or Dad did that made you feel loved?
- If you could tell Future You one thing, what would it be?
- What do you hope next year feels like?

## Favorite Things (expanded "Current Favorites")
Favorite: Book · Movie · Song · Game · Food · Animal · Place · Toy · Outfit · Family
Tradition · Holiday · Bible Verse (optional) · Joke · Dessert · Thing We Learned ·
Field Trip · Subject · Thing About Homeschool · Dream Vacation.

## Tiny Masterpieces (was Drawings) — with prompts
"This piece reminds me of…" / "I made this because…"

## Tiny Moments (new page — a favorite idea)
A single page of little one-liners; "not every memory needs a whole page."
Format: "Today… I lost my first tooth. Caught a butterfly. Built a fort. Made
pancakes. Learned fractions. Read under a blanket. Played in the rain." Closing line:
"Twenty years later… those are everything."

## Adventure Pages (categories, not random photos)
Group adventures into named categories: Our Favorite Field Trip · Nature Adventures ·
Kitchen Creations · Science Experiments · Books That Took Us Somewhere · Community Days
· Helping Others · Christmas Memories · Summer Adventures · Rainy Day Fun.

## Look How Much I've Grown (was "Wins")
Rename to "Look How Much I've Grown." Examples: first chapter book · rode a bike · swam
underwater · tied my shoes · learned multiplication · made a new friend · started piano
· learned to cook eggs.

## Things I Never Want to Forget About You Right Now (new — parent → child)
Your laugh… The way you pronounce… Your bedtime questions… Your favorite stuffed
animal… The songs you sing… Your little voice… The hugs… The freckles… The missing
tooth… (This is the page parents cry over.)

## Open When You're Grown (new — letter to future child)
> Dear Future {Child}, Right now you are… One thing I hope never changes… One thing I
> hope you always remember… One thing I hope you chase… Love, Mom & Dad

## Until Next Year… (new closing page)
> Every homeschool year is different. Some lessons were easy. Some were messy. Some
> happened around the kitchen table. Some happened outside under the sky. Some weren't
> in any curriculum at all. Thank you for filling these pages with laughter, curiosity,
> kindness, and love. Here's to another year of growing together.

## Signature feature — One Question a Month
Brittany's big idea, and a real differentiator. Each month, the family answers ONE
question (only one). Examples: What made you laugh this month? What challenged you?
What surprised you? What are you thankful for? What was your favorite day? What's
something you'll never forget?
At year-end, the twelve answers appear together as a designed month-by-month spread —
"Our year, month by month" — in the family's own words (NO AI). They've been collecting
the story all year, so there's no June scramble to remember it.
Why it matters: most homeschool tools capture academics; this captures childhood.
It's the keepsake families read together decades later, and a strong reason to choose
(and stay with) Rooted.

## What's copy vs. what's a new feature
- Mostly copy (fast): TOC names, cover subtitle, Letter prompt, "A Day We'll Never
  Forget" labels + caption prompts, Year-End Conversation questions, "Look How Much
  I've Grown," "Until Next Year" closing, expanded Favorite Things, Tiny Masterpieces
  prompts.
- New pages needing new input fields + capture UI: "This Was {Child}" snapshot, "Tiny
  Moments," "Things I Never Want to Forget," "Open When You're Grown," Adventure
  categories.
- New ongoing feature: One Question a Month (a monthly prompt surface + a year-end
  month-by-month recap page).

## Capture matters as much as display
Many of these are prompts a family fills in. They need a home to enter answers (the
yearbook edit/guided flow), and the monthly question needs a once-a-month prompt on the
Today page (gentle, dismissible). A gorgeous empty page no one filled in isn't a
keepsake — the guided input is half the feature.

## Suggested build waves
1. Wave 1 — copy & rename (fast, high payoff, low risk): TOC names, cover subtitle,
   Letter prompt + "Love,", "A Day We'll Never Forget," caption prompts, Year-End
   Conversation questions, "Look How Much I've Grown," "Until Next Year" closing,
   expanded Favorite Things, Tiny Masterpieces prompts. Mostly labels + reusing fields.
2. Wave 2 — new keepsake pages (new fields + capture): "This Was {Child}," "Tiny
   Moments," "Things I Never Want to Forget," "Open When You're Grown," Adventure
   categories.
3. Wave 3 — One Question a Month: monthly prompt on Today + the year-end month-by-month
   recap page.

All of it flows through the saved yearbook model, renders identically in reader and
PDF, and stays no-AI.
